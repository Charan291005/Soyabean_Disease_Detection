Project Overview

Designed for field deployment, not just laboratory benchmarking

Soybean disease inspection is difficult to scale manually because field conditions shift constantly, symptoms overlap, and imaging quality varies across devices. This prototype presents the workflow as an operational monitoring platform rather than a static classifier demo.

Manual inspection bottlenecks

Visual scoring in real farms is labor-intensive, subjective, and sensitive to lighting, occlusion, and crop growth stage.

Overlapping symptom patterns

Brown Spot, Septoria Leaf Spot, and Frogeye Leaf Spot can show visually similar lesions, requiring consistent model support.

Multi-source practicality

Smartphone capture supports agronomists, UAV imaging expands coverage, and IoT cameras enable repeat monitoring at fixed locations.

Deployment-aware AI workflow

The system emphasizes preprocessing, source-aware inference, cloud aggregation, and decision support to reflect real operational use.

Model Insights

Classification and localization models aligned with deployment constraints

This section frames model choice in research terms, balancing performance metrics with latency, memory, and operational suitability.

Why ResNet works here

ResNet supports stable image-level disease classification by preserving deep feature learning without vanishing-gradient collapse. It is well suited to mobile capture workflows and benchmark-driven class discrimination.

Why YOLO matters here

YOLO provides lesion localization, real-time detection, and region-aware visualization that strengthens supervisor demos and field deployment credibility, especially for UAV and edge use cases.

Why the pairing is practical

A hybrid setup allows quick local triage, spatial lesion awareness, and cloud-side aggregation. This reflects a realistic path toward edge/mobile/UAV readiness rather than a single-model lab demo.
